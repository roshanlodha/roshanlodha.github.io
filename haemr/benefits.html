What Happens to Each Policy When You Leave MGB

Today → Residency End (~2029)
Both coverages active
Group LTD is free and covers 60% of the PGY salary (~$4,229/mo gross, taxable). An individual policy can layer on top of that base coverage during training.
Day You Leave MGB
Group LTD ends with employment
The moment MGB employment ends, the group LTD policy ends. A conversion option typically exists but is expensive with inferior terms. Pre-existing conditions developed during residency may be excluded from a new employer's group plan.
Attending Year 1 — Same Day
Guardian IDI stays active through job changes
If an individual policy was purchased during residency, it remains active through job changes. The FIO rider allows benefit increases as income rises, without new medical underwriting, at rates tied to the original issue date.
Any Point During Career
Definition differences become more material over time
If an injury prevents hands-on EM care, a true own-occupation policy can continue paying even if the physician later works in teaching, consulting, or administration. That distinction is especially meaningful for physically demanding, procedural specialties.

Guardian IDI Key Features

FeatureWhat It MeansWhy It Matters for EM
GSI — Guaranteed Standard Issue Key Coverage guaranteed — no exam, labs, or underwriting Access is available during training without exams or labs. Applying after a health change may result in exclusions, higher rates, or denial
Noncancelable Rider Key Guardian cannot raise premiums or reduce coverage while premiums are paid Shows how an individual policy can keep the original premium structure while employer group terms may change over time
True Own-Occupation (EM) Key Disabled if unable to perform EM specialty duties — even while working in another field Especially relevant in physically active, procedural specialties such as EM
FIO — Future Increase Option Increase benefit every 3 years based on income growth — no new medical underwriting Allows scaling from resident benefit levels to attending income without new medical underwriting
90-Day Elimination Period Must be disabled 90 days before benefits begin MGB's 12-week salary continuation roughly lines up with this gap during residency; after residency the gap has to be self-funded
Benefit Period: To Age 65/67 Benefits paid for entire disability duration — potentially 30+ years A long-duration disability early in practice can create decades of replacement-income needs
COLA Rider (Optional) Annual 3% compound benefit increase during disability Without COLA, $7,500/mo at age 35 is worth ~$3,100/mo in today's dollars after 30 years
Partial/Residual Disability 15% income loss triggers partial benefits — covers reduced-hours work Many disabilities are partial. A partial injury forcing reduced shifts still generates a payout
Premium Waiver Premiums waived while collecting disability benefits Policy stays active during a disability without paying premiums
Graded vs. Level Premiums Graded starts lower and increases annually; level is fixed forever Graded is cheaper during residency (~$80–120/mo vs. ~$150–200/mo level), while level holds premiums flat. Breakeven is often around year 10, and graded can later be converted to level
30% GME Discount Permanent 30% discount through the MGB residency program Permanent — applies forever. Lost if applying after leaving GME status
Portability Individual policy owned by the insured — not the employer Survives every job change and career transition. Group LTD ends at MGB; this one does not
🚨Application sequencing note: Guardian's GSI offer at MGB requires applying with Guardian first before other disability carriers. Applying elsewhere first (Standard, Ameritas, Principal) forfeits eligibility for the no-underwriting GSI program.
📞Reference link: The resident-specific Guardian pathway is summarized at residentgme.com. If comparing quotes, keep the benefit amount, riders, elimination period, and disability definition constant across carriers.
💰Timing note: Resident discounts and GSI availability are time-sensitive program features. The same coverage structure may price differently later and may require full underwriting instead of guaranteed issue.
