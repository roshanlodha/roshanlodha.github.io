Non-random Rotation Matching Algorithm

How it works

The Non-random Rotation Matching Algorithm (NRMA) is designed to give you more control over your clerkship schedule. Instead of a random lottery, we use an optimization algorithm to match students based on their expressed preferences.

The "Bean" System

You are given 24 beans to distribute across the available rotation orders.

  • Express Strength: Assign more beans to the options you prefer most.
  • Flexibility: You can put all 24 beans on one option, or distribute them (e.g., 12, 6, 3, 3) if you are flexible.
  • Optimal Outcome: The algorithm finds the assignment that maximizes the total "bean value" across the entire class, ensuring the fairest possible distribution.

The Rotations

There are four rotation orders available. You will be assigned to one of these based on your preferences:

  • Option 1: LAB → TBC2 → TBC3 → TBC1
  • Option 2: TBC2 → LAB → TBC1 → TBC3
  • Option 3: TBC3 → TBC1 → LAB → TBC2
  • Option 4: TBC1 → TBC3 → TBC2 → LAB

Instructions

  1. Review the rotation options listed in the form.
  2. Allocate your 24 beans among the options.
  3. Ensure your total adds up to exactly 24.
  4. Submit the form before the deadline.
