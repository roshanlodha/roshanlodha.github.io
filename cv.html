Work and Research Experience

  • Cancer Data Science Lab, NIH, Bethesda, MD
    • 2024 - present
    • Mentor: Eytan Ruppin, M.D., Ph.D.
    • Conducted research at the NIH in machine learning and oncology. Co-developed Path2Space, an attention-based deep learning model to predict spatial transcriptomics directly from histology slides. Discovered a mathematical spatial biomarker significantly associated with treatment response in breast cancer. Independently designed and built a companion web platform to democritize model availability. Currently leading a project extension focused on predicting single-cell resolution cell types, aiming for higher fidelity than current human annotations.
  • Step 1 Tutor at HyGuru LLC
    • 2024 - present
    • Reference: Rahul Damania, M.D.
    • Tutored medical students preparing for USMLE Step 1. Designed individualized study plans, guided content review, and provided test-taking strategies. Focused on integrating spaced repetition, performance tracking, and high-yield concepts. Supported students through exam stress and schedule adjustments. Designed and integrated software tools to enhance learning efficiency, including NBMEretester and Anki.
  • Chung Lab, Cleveland Clinic Foundation
    • 2021-present
    • Mentors: Mina Chung, M.D. and John Barnard, Ph.D.
    • Conducted machine learning research in cardiology. Conducted literature review to identify key genetic contributors to AF recurrence. Correlated mathematically defined metafeatures extracted from segmented cardiac CT/MRI with specific genetic SNPs associated with atrial fibrillation. Additionally led a project focusing on developing a 3D convolutional neural network (3D-CNN) to predict post-ablation atrial fibrillation recurrence using pre-ablation CT scans. Learned transferable skills like image augmentation and radiology basics.
  • Gladson Lab, Lerner Research Institute
    • 2021-2024
    • Mentor: Candece Gladson, M.D.
    • Led a bioinformatics project analyzing bulk RNA-seq data to stratify glioblastoma patients by response to bevacizumab. Used differential gene expression and survival analysis to identify response-associated signatures. Identified EGR1 as a key driver mediator in non-response to bevacizumab therapy. Solely designed and executed the project; first-author publication currently under review. Through this, learned about management of lab resources, scientific writing, and navigating the manuscript submission/review process.
  • Feng Lab, University of California, San Francisco
    • 2018-2021
    • Mentors: Jonathan Chou, M.D., Ph.D. and Felix Feng, M.D.
    • Conducted research in prostate cancer genomics. Through benchtop techniques (western blot, qPCR, and cell culture) explored how CDK12 loss affects DNA repair pathways and response to PARP inhibitors. Used a CRISPRi screen to explore how synthetic lethality can be exploited in CDK12-deficient prostate cancer.
